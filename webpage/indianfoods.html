Indian Foods

North Indian Cuisine

North Indian cuisine is known for its rich and diverse flavors, influenced by Mughlai and Persian culinary traditions. It includes dishes like Butter Chicken, Palak Paneer, and Rogan Josh, which are characterized by their use of aromatic spices and creamy gravies.

Popular bread varieties such as Naan and Paratha complement these dishes, along with staple ingredients like rice and lentils.

South Indian Cuisine

South Indian cuisine is known for its extensive use of rice, lentils, coconut, and spices, resulting in flavorful and aromatic dishes. Staples like Dosa, Idli, and Sambar are popular across the region and are often served with coconut chutney and tangy tamarind sauce.

Coconut-based curries, such as Coconut Fish Curry and Avial, are also prominent in South Indian cuisine, reflecting the coastal influence on culinary traditions.

East Indian Cuisine

East Indian cuisine is characterized by its use of rice, fish, and various indigenous vegetables and spices. Dishes like Machher Jhol (fish curry), Rosogolla (a syrupy dessert), and Litti Chokha (roasted wheat balls with mashed vegetables) are popular in this region.

Mustard oil is commonly used for cooking, and sweets made from milk and jaggery are enjoyed as desserts.

West Indian Cuisine

West Indian cuisine is diverse, influenced by Portuguese, British, and native Indian cultures. It features dishes like Vada Pav (potato fritter sandwich), Bombil Fry (crispy Bombay duck), and Bhakri (sorghum flatbread).

Coconut, seafood, and spicy chutneys are common ingredients, reflecting the coastal geography of the region.
